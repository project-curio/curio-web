Our mission

Curio elevates reflections over ratings so cultural experiences feel personal, contextual, and inclusive.

We help visitors discover places through stories that honor feeling, access, and community care — while giving institutions tools to share their values.

Built by museum practitioners, designers, and community archivists, Curio prioritizes accessibility, data transparency, and joyful discovery at every step.

Founded 2024 · Prototype City

Independent public interest studio led by cultural sector veterans.

Focus Reflection-first discovery

Centering qualitative insights, accessibility, and institutional transparency.

Stage Front-end MVP

Why “reflections, not reviews”?

Traditional review platforms reward hot takes. Cultural experiences deserve nuance. Curio’s guided prompts invite visitors to articulate how a place made them feel, who it served, and what they would recommend.

Belonging

Curiosity should feel welcoming — whether you are a first-time museum visitor, a disabled researcher, or a family exploring together.
We collaborate with community advisors and access consultants to keep the platform hospitable.

Transparency

Institutions deserve space to articulate mission, access notes, and interpretive choices alongside visitor reflections.
Everything from moderation practices to data stewardship lives in plain language dashboards.

Reciprocity

Reflections travel back to cultural partners so they can adapt programming, celebrate staff labor, and share impact stories.
We design in partnership with museums, gardens, archives, and artist-run spaces.

Team & advisory cadence

Curio’s core team spans exhibition design, civic tech, and accessibility advocacy. An advisory council of museum educators, archivists, and community organizers meets quarterly to stress-test features.

Core studio

Accessibility & data commitments

We design with WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline, layering on inclusive research, community review, and transparent governance.

Accessibility first

Interface verified through automated tooling, manual audits, and user testing with disabled cultural workers.

Consent-centered data

Reflections are anonymized by default, with opt-in attribution. Institutions receive aggregated insights, not personal data.
